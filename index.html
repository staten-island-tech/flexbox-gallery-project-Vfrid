Paul Cezzane

Paul Cezanne is a famous artist of the post-impressionist movement, creating life in his paintings of still objects through the use of brush strokes and unorthodox color choices.

Dasha Shishkin

Shishkin conjures up life-like yet abstract worlds where it is hard to discern who is who. The figures are in perpetual movement, hovering and floating along in front of and behind each other.

Van Gogh

Van Gogh's landscape paintings provoke feelings of tranquility through the bold patterned brush strokes and bright colors. The trees are dark against a bright twilight sky, creating stark contrast that draws attention to their imposing figures.

Van Gogh

Rembrandt

Christ in the Storm on the Sea of Galilee is a 1633 oil-on-canvas painting by the Dutch Golden Age painter Rembrandt van Rijn. It is classified as a historical painting and is among the largest and earliest of Rembrandt's works. It was purchased by Bernard Berenson for Isabella Stewart Gardner in 1869 and was displayed at the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum in Boston before its theft in 1990; it remains missing.
